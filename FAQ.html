FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: What is ragging?

Ragging is an act of teasing, tormenting, or harassing new students in educational institutions. It is strictly prohibited and illegal in many places.

Q: How can I report ragging?

You can report ragging by using our "Raise Complaint" service. Visit the "Services" section for more information.

Q: Is ragging punishable by law?

Yes, ragging is punishable by law in many countries. It can lead to serious consequences, including imprisonment.

Q: What are the penalties for ragging?

Penalties for ragging can include suspension, expulsion, fines, or even legal actions. The severity of penalties varies depending on the institution and the nature of the ragging.

Q: Is there a way to report ragging anonymously?

Yes, we offer an anonymous reporting feature on our portal to protect your identity while reporting ragging incidents. Your safety is our priority.

Q: How can I raise awareness about ragging?

You can raise awareness about ragging by participating in anti-ragging campaigns, workshops, and educating others about its consequences. You can also use social media to spread the message against ragging.

Q: How can parents ensure their child's safety from ragging?

Parents should have open communication with their children about the risks of ragging, encourage them to report any incidents, and support their educational institution's anti-ragging policies.
